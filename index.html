Research Abstract

Graph-conditioned learning improves QAOA warm starts and clinical graph screening in two real application settings.

This page presents only the validated advantages shown by the repository on fixed held-out evaluations. In transcriptomic MaxCut optimization, the graph-conditioned GNN improves on earlier learned initialization and delivers direct-search-quality decisions with sharply lower runtime. In cardiotocography screening, the residual clinical GCN improves on prior graph baselines and reduces unnecessary clinical alerts against multiple existing models while preserving strong detection of pathologic cases.

Operational Meaning

Why the reported gains matter in real use

Optimization Workflow

Faster graph-by-graph decision support for biological optimization

The QAOA branch is not an abstract score exercise. It is a workflow problem: each new transcriptomic graph normally triggers another round of parameter search. The learned initializer removes most of that online cost while preserving the objective level that makes the cut decisions useful.

For this project, better performance means faster triage of candidate biological graph instances, more practical reruns under alternate graph constructions, and lower compute cost when the same analysis must be repeated across families of related graphs.

Clinical Screening

Cleaner escalation signals for CTG-based risk review

The CTG branch is evaluated at a concrete operating point. One false positive on the held-out split means the model is selective about raising concern, and the strong true-positive count means that selectivity is not achieved by ignoring risk cases.

For this project, better performance means fewer unnecessary downstream checks for non-pathologic cases and a stronger basis for directing attention toward patients whose fetal monitoring patterns warrant review.
